Mass sensitivity of individual air-shower observables is described in the context of state-of-the-art air-shower models presented in~\Cref{chap:airshowers}.
Statistically, there is an average correlation between individual observables and cosmic-ray mass; however, also described is the stochastic nature of the particle interactions within the air shower, resulting in shower-to-shower fluctuations of air-shower observables between primary cosmic rays of the same initial parameters.
Shower-to-shower fluctuations limit the separative power of individual observables for cosmic-ray mass, complicating mass determination of single events.
Yet, simultaneously combining multiple, mass-sensitive, air-shower observables can improve the mass separation power at event level.
Improvements in mass separation when studying multiple observables have been shown in both simulations and preliminary data analyses, highlighting the science potential for combining multiple observables in cosmic-ray analyses. CITATIONS

This chapter presents the results of a mass sensitivity study of individual air-shower observables, and their combination, from cosmic-ray air showers simulated with CORSIKA (COsmic Ray SImulations for KAscade). 
All analysis is performed using a general purpose software framework for studying the cosmic-ray mass separation of observables.
The mantra of this framework is to study the intrinsic potential of air-shower observables for event-by-event cosmic-ray mass separation.
