\chapter{The Pierre Auger Observatory}\label{chap:auger}

The Pierre Auger Observatory is located near the town of Malarg\"ue in the Mendoza province of Argentina between the Andes mountain range and la Pampa Amarilla.
Auger, as the observatory is commonly referred to, is the world's largest cosmic-ray observatory, covering a total area of \qty{3000}{\kilo\meter^{2}}.
The observatory detects air showers initiated by ultra-high-energy cosmic rays, the highest energy particles in the known Universe and whose origin remains unknown.
It is not only the world's largest cosmic-ray observatory, but adding to the novelty is the hybrid detection mode of Auger.
The observatory consists of two primary detectors, the surface detector (SD), an array of over 1600 water-Cherenkov detectors (WCDs) spread across the full \qty{3000}{\kilo\meter^{2}} footprint of the observatory, and a fluorescence detector (FD), 24 fluorescence telescopes overlooking the SD array at four locations around the array edges.
